Key principles

Assessment: The transcript is the work. The artifact is evidence the work concluded. Grading the artifact alone is invalid. Each prompt is a node; the conversation is the traversal; the artifact is one path's endpoint, not the map.

Writing with AI: Two distinct moments — (1) prompting, where thinking becomes language; (2) finishing, where the student adjusts word choice, flow, verb tense. Any external editing must return to the conversation before closing.

Authorial intention: The condition that makes output judgeable. Without it, there is no basis for evaluation.

"Slop": Prose that performs relatability or expertise without earning it. Fluency ≠ literacy. Simulated intimacy is a distinct failure mode.

Framework integrity: "Living with AI" is program framing, not a fourth pillar. RTW remains the three-part definition. "AI Literacy" is the umbrella term; "generative AI literacy" specifies current pedagogical methods when needed.

MICA (Monadic Inference Cycle Analysis): Framework for analyzing AI conversation archives. Each prompt-response cycle is a discrete unit. Grounded in Latour/Tarde monadological approaches. RTW rubric dimensions: responsiveness to model output, directional correction, conceptual deepening, prompt precision over time, evidence of reading between prompts.
