AI Literacy Curriculum

Reading, thinking, and writing with the tools, techniques, and technological systems consistent with one's peers.

This site holds the working materials of an AI literacy curriculum developed at SUNY Polytechnic Institute. Two scaffolds are in use: a content-neutral Learner's Permit covering foundational generative AI literacy, and a more advanced Commercial License covering agentic AI operation.

The transcripts of the conversations that produced these scaffolds are also archived here. The transcript is the work; the artifact is evidence the work concluded.

About the framework

The curriculum treats AI as cognitive infrastructure, not subject matter. Students develop literacy through AI, not about it. Every exercise produces two artifacts: the model's final response and the full transcript of the exchange that produced it. The transcript is the primary evidence of thinking; the artifact is evidence the work concluded.

Using this resource

These curriculum scaffolds were built through extensive conversation with Claude about the goals of each exercise, and they embed a particular vision of AI literacy: students should read, think, and write with AI tools purposively, developing habits of validation and verification that buttress their use. The scaffolds are also designed to be generative. The HTML files and transcript .md files can be uploaded to any LLM to produce your own curriculum variants or exercises. The resulting HTML artifacts can be uploaded directly to Brightspace or equivalent LMS.

The hosted version of this curriculum is at stevesunypoly.github.io/ai-literacy-2026/. Source files are available at github.com/stevesunypoly/ai-literacy-2026.
